About me

I am a Master’s student in Computer Science at the Technical University of Munich. Before that, I spent a few wonderful years in the small town of Saarbrücken, where I completed my Bachelor’s at the University of Saarland.

My research interests are two-fold, driven by a passion for interesting theory and mathematically grounded analysis:

  1. Machine Learning Optimization & Learning Dynamics. Particularly the phenomenon of the Edge of Stability and implicit regularisation, alongside sparsity in overparameterized models, and more generally optimization theory, deep learning theory, and statistical learning theory.
  2. Neural Operators. FNOs, Laplace Neural Operators, and neural operators for fluids.

I currently work with Rebekka Burkholz and Tom Jacobs at the Relational-ML lab at CISPA on deep learning theory. Previously, I worked in Fabian Theis's lab at the Institute of Computational Biology (Helmholtz), where I built a toolbox unifying flow matching and optimal transport methods for single-cell biology into a single, unified framework.

Additionally, I also work on neural operators with Karnik Ram at the CVG group (Dr. Daniel Cremers) at TUM.

During my Bachelor’s, I was fortunate to work with Tatjana Chavdarova on spectral algorithms for min-max (game) optimization.
